ATF Fluids

Developed to help prevent leaks, maximize transmission performance, reduce transmission wear, and maintain smooth shifting longer than conventional fluids, Valvoline™ Multi-Vehicle ATF is a full synthetic formulation with advanced additives to prevent the major causes of transmission breakdown and help extend transmission life.

Valvoline™ Multi-Vehicle ATF is one of a complete line of ATF fluids Valvoline™ offers.

Selling Points

Select eachto learn more.

Valvoline™ Multi-Vehicle ATF is Valvoline’s™ most versatile transmission fluid. It’s suitable for use in over 90% of vehicles in operation with traditional automatic transmissions.
Valvoline™ Multi-Vehicle ATF is recommended for most Ford, GM, Toyota, Lexus, Honda, Acura, Nissan, Mazda, Mitsubishi, Hyundai, BMW, Volkswagen, Audi, and Mercedes models (except those with CVTs, or those calling for Type F ATF).
Valvoline™ has made sales and inventory management for an installer easier as they can consolidate their ATF inventory from multiple SKUs down to one product—Valvoline™ Multi-Vehicle ATF. This reduces their carrying costs while providing their customers with a higher level of transmission protection.
With such a high rate of transmission acceptability, Valvoline™ Multi-Vehicle ATF helps technicians avoid costly mistakes by putting the wrong fluid type into a transmission. It greatly lessens misapplication risks.

Features and Benefits

Select eachto learn more.

Developed after extensive in-house testing, independent lab reviews, and field-testing support.
Formulated with premium synthetic base stocks, long-life friction modifiers, special anti-wear additives, and shear stable viscosity modifiers. It helps a transmission maximize its efficiency and performance.
Benefits include:
  • A full synthetic formulation with advanced additive technology
  • High-performance seal conditioners maintain and preserve the elasticity of seals to help prevent leaks
  • Smoother shifting, elimination of slippage and shudder, and the prevention of clutch wear
  • Reduced varnish formation
  • Better oil flow at low temperatures and greater film protection at higher temperatures
  • Extending the life of a transmission
  • Improved thermal and oxidation performance
  • Excellent torque transfer
