Dealership Departments

The departments in any dealership, regardless of its size, have specific, symbiotic relationships. Each relies on the other to achieve their business goals.

While you won’t be interacting with each department, it’s important to understand how they interact with each other.

Understanding the roles and relationships of each department in a dealership can give you insight into how a dealership can holistically benefit from Valvoline™ products and services.

Dealerships consist of two distinct businesses.

The Front End of a dealership is where you can find the Sales Team and the Finance and Insurance Department. This part of a dealership is also referred to as Variable Operations.

The Back End of a dealership consists of the Parts Department and Service Department. This part of a dealership is also referred to as Fixed Operations.

FINANCE AND INSURANCE

When a customer has found the car they like, the Finance and Insurance (F&I) team will step in. They will help arrange financing (loans) for the customer. In many ways, the Finance and Insurance team acts as an extension of the sales staff as they work to get customers to add warranties and various automotive package upgrades at the time of closing.

SALES

A new customer will usually start their dealership experience by walking in the front door to buy a car. Depending on the size of a dealership, new and used sales may be combined here, or there may be two separate areas for them.

The profitability of this department is variable because it can be affected by vehicle availability, competitor pricing, the knowledgeability of the sales staff, the economy, the supply chain, and even the weather!

PARTS DEPARTMENT

The Parts Department has to satisfy two different types of customers.

  1. The Service Department – The Parts team has to make sure that the Service team has everything they need to maintain and repair customer cars.
  2. Auto Repair Shops – Local mechanics will often turn to a dealership’s Parts department to obtain OEM products for their customers’ work.

Revenue in the Parts and Service Departments tends to be more consistent, or fixed, over the course of a year.

SERVICE DEPARTMENT

The Service team is responsible for keeping cars that come through the dealership on the road. This means that before a used car can be placed on the lot, a Service team member will inspect it and make necessary repairs.

Customers will use the Service Department to have warranty-driven work completed.

The Service Manager also wants to increase the flow of customers who come in for non-warranty service and repairs.

As a representative at Valvoline™, the Service Department will be your main point of contact.
